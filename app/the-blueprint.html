Lesson 7: Break of Structure

If a trend is broken, e.g. the highest low gets past by a candle, it's called a break of structure (BOS).

Break of Structure: when price breaks below the previous higher low
Tip
If there are many candles the same colour all in a row, see them as one candle. Break of structure happens often. They happen with every trend.

Lesson 8: Liquidity Part 1

What is Liquidity?
Testing orders. It exists on every timeframe. It is required for the market to move and change direction. It acts like a magnet. Where price wants to go = charging towards liquidity.

Why do we need it? It determines where we are going towards and it is required for the market to move and change direction.

Liquidity is where lots of orders get filled close to highs. Lots of orders that get stopped out. They got trapped at a price, mostly higher or lower. People who are chasing the market need to be on the right side.

Liquidity sweep: price takes out stops above highs, then reverses

Lesson 8 (continued): Liquidity for Downtrends

Some things good for downtrends: possibility that people are going to be shorting and placing stops below lows. The reasons we choose to fill all the orders is where we see the structure.

Got low going, got swept out, shows it was good enough to go long in the opposite direction. Strong buy signal, showing it is going to be moving to the upside.

Lesson 9: Liquidity Part 2

Same concept from the sell side perspective. Liquidity rests above highs (buy side) and below lows (sell side).

Day 4

Lesson 10: Order Blocks

Definition: A price range where large orders were placed, causing the market to change direction.

Why do we want to use it?
If large smart money orders were able to be filled, there will be more orders, so we know price will likely react and move in the same direction as those orders. More orders filled = safe to assume it will continue.
Order Block: price returns to zone where large orders were filled, then continues

Lesson 11: Order Blocks Part 2

Where do large orders get filled? Where liquidity rests. We know this by break of structure. Can be revisited in the opposite direction.

Lesson 12: Fair Value Gaps and Imbalances

What are gaps and imbalances?
An imbalance of orders where the price surface of the market lacks orders in the opposite direction. It helps predict where the market wants to go.

Lesson 13: Gaps and Imbalances Part 2

Can be identified by 3 candles.

Fair Value Gap: the gap between the wick of Candle 1 and the wick of Candle 3
What is NOT a Fair Value Gap
When the wicks from the first and third candle meet in the body of the big candle. No gap = no FVG.

Lesson 12 (continued): Balanced Price Action

When price exceeds the FVG, this is disrespected. It is no longer valid. But if an FVG is respected, we can then use it to make a trade.

If we only have 3 imbalances/FVGs but only one gets filled, the price is no longer doing what it needs to balance. Price returned = no longer needs to balance out the imbalances.

When Confluence Doesn't Work:
  • New market opens (London session, New York session, Asian session)
  • Big NEWS event happens
Once price balances out, there is no need for it to continue filling the other FVGs. Price is actually going to continue on its original trend.

Lesson 14: Inverse Fair Value Gaps (IFVG)

Notation on a fair value gap. If we see a confluence close to the entry, it can be a reason to take a trade. Use it as a sign for a potential trade setup. Don't use it as a standalone tool. Use it close to FVG.

When price breaks past a FVG, it becomes an inverse FVG. Safe to assume it will continue in its own trend.

Lesson 15: Balanced Price Action (BPR)

Combining areas: fair value gaps to create imbalance in a predicted range. The part where the two FVGs cross over is what we are looking for.

Balanced Price Range: where bullish and bearish FVGs overlap. This is the entry zone.

Not a standalone reason to trade. Another confluence to follow. But using both FVGs (BPR) gives more confluence to buy or sell.

Lesson 16: Equilibrium Part 1

It measures the point from swing high to swing low (or vice versa), identifying where price is in a premium and a discount. It identifies a premium and discounted price as continuation confluence.

Lesson 17: Equilibrium Part 2

Equilibrium: if buying power comes out of the discount zone, assume continuation up

Lesson 18: Breaker Blocks Part 1

Definition: A structure opposite of the current trend, which creates a price range with buy/sell orders depending on the previous trend.

If price revisits that area, we can assume that if there is selling/buying pressure out of it, price will continue in that direction due to the lack of orders from the previous trend.

Lesson 19: Breaker Blocks Part 2

Daily breaker blocks = lack of buy orders on the other side.

Lesson 20: SMT Divergence

First step to find the bias for the day. Is it bullish or bearish?

When one index is forcing what another is going to do. NQ and ES. NQ follows about 90% of the time.

What to Look For:
  • Bullish: Higher low formed on one index and a lower low on the other
  • Bearish: High formed on one and a higher high on the other
SMT Divergence: when ES and NQ make different structures at the same point
Key Rule
Look for SMT Divergences on either the 5 minute or the 15 minute chart. If we see one market go down or up and it confirms, we then know the bias for the rest of the market.

Day 7: Psychology

What is Psychology?

  • The mindset you have when approaching trading, business, life
  • The way problems arise and how you think about them
  • The way you either emotionally react, or with ease, or with no problem

Why Do We Need Psychology?

  • If we approach trading the wrong way, we are going into it with the wrong results
  • Being good at trading means being able to predict price action with high probability
  • If we think about problems the wrong way, we get a result we do not want
  • If we act emotionally or off of instinct, we will get a different result than the one we desired
Psychology is the driver of action. If you think the wrong way, you will act the wrong way. If you think the right way, you will act the right way.

Understanding Psychology

There is only two ways to approach it:

  1. Do what you are supposed to because you know that it will produce the desired result
  2. Just do what you are supposed to, to get a wanted result

Manifestation: Act on what you think.

Shiny Object Syndrome

  • Focus more on what you should NOT be doing, rather than what you should do
  • Reverse think: from end result, and back in time through actions to the start
  • Go from start to beginning, rather than getting overwhelmed
The second you make money is like a feeling. By focusing on things I should be doing, I can remove them, and I will make me a better trader. I will make more profit over time.

Psychology Part 2: Review

  • Psychology is the mindset you have when approaching trading, business, life
  • The way you think about failure and risk: the way they arise, and the way you act
  • We need psychology in order to do actions that provide us with the desired results
  • We must know how to think. By controlling how we think, we subconsciously control how we act

Thinking Long Term

How long do you want trading to change your life? Fine. Doesn't matter. All I want is success, and that doesn't happen in an instant.

If you want trading to change your life, it won't happen easily. You must want to master it. Notice the wording: "trading, I want to master." There is no timeline for when you will be ready. Which is why we need practice.

Applying It

  • If you love trading so much to the point where you can do it forever, you will be successful without making a dollar
  • Treat it like a game and get really good at it
  • Stop putting time/money restrictions on what should be enjoyable
  • Become obsessed with winning trades, not making money
  • Become obsessed with improving
Start with something easier: A bad habit that you have been wanting to kick but haven't had the balls to do it. If you can do this, you can switch how you think about trading.

Do something even easier: Do nothing for 30 minutes in silence in the dark. Understand love and patience.

Effort = Results

The time and effort you put in = results out. If you put nothing in, you'll just waste money and time. You will make your money back. Skills take 10,000 hours to master.

Have fun. If you are not having fun, it's not for you. If trading sounds fun, that sounds like you can work in the market forever.

Liquidity (Detailed)

Buy Orders: In an uptrend, there are 2 types of buyers:

  1. Someone who took a short, got stopped out, and is now trying to buy back
  2. Someone who is entering a trade above the previous high

Sell Orders: Same thing. Those that rest below lows.

The Market Makers (AKA "The Devils")

  • They have a massive amount of capital
  • With a lot of capital, they can't enter with a million dollar order in one direction
  • To buy a share, someone has to be willing to sell it
  • This is why we get liquidity sweeps

Imbalances / FVG (Detailed)

  • Imbalances and FVGs are a 3-candle stick pattern
  • It shows a lack of orders within the market
  • Due to the lack of sell orders, the market is going to want to revisit that area, then pushing the market back up even higher

Balanced Price Action (Detailed)

When price exceeds the FVG, it is disrespected. No longer valid. If we only have 3 FVGs but only one gets filled, price is no longer doing what it needs to balance.

Think of balanced out price action almost as a shield.

IFVG (Detailed)

An IFVG is basically like an FVG, but when the FVG gets disrespected. It can be used as an indicator. IFVG works really well with, or after BOS and IFVG: both show the same thing, disrespect of order flow.

Multiple FVGs: If there are more than 1 FVG, one is disrespected but the second one is respected, this is not an IFVG for the second one. For it to be multiple FVGs, all must be disrespected.

When price disrespects an FVG with multiple FVGs, we end the shortest FVG, unless in the creation of the IFVG, there was a retracement back into the imbalance, balancing that price range out.

Break of Structure (Detailed)

Need to be able to identify on highs and lows:

  • High: an up candle and a down candle
  • Low: a down candle and an up candle

To find break of structure: find what must have marked out the highs and lows, find the point where price has exceeded past our previous lows (if bullish), telling us it wants to go in the opposite direction.

Order Blocks (Detailed)

Order blocks and breaker blocks are very similar. They are pretty much the opposite of each other. Don't get confused by them. KISS: Keep It Simple.

An order block is a price concept where a significant amount of orders were placed. It is one formed when the market sweeps on liquidity, ending or causing a break of structure.

3 Things to Look For in an Order Block

1. Liquidity Sweep
2. Break of Structure
3. Order Block

What is a Breaker Block?

It is the leading up prior to the liquidity sweep. It is the price action right before the sweep of liquidity.

SMT Divergence (Detailed)

It is basically a way to tell the true direction of the market. ES follows New York highs, but NQ 90% of the time follows that buy.

Order Flow

If we are in an uptrend and we see people who buy levels, and we see an FVG, that is a really good clue to where the market works.

Steps: FVG getting disrespected (IFVG), then look for continuation of new order flow.

Fibonacci Extension

It is a confluence that can be used interchangeably with IFVG or break of structure. Not a serious confluence, just a bit of extra knowledge. Tyler uses it as a point to see a break of structure, to see where price is going to bounce.

Note
IFVG and BOS take priority over Fibonacci. TJR doesn't really recommend to use this.

Draws on Liquidity and Key Levels

2 Different Types of Liquidity:

High Resistance Liquidity

A high or a low that has already been swept. Liquidity taken. Harder to take out because it's more resistance.

Low Resistance Liquidity

A group of highs or lows that hasn't been broken out yet. Also known as trendline liquidity. Basically just a trend of highs or lows.

Price is more likely to take the low resistance liquidity and continue with its current order flow. Low resistance is easier to take out.

Most Important Draws on Liquidity

  • Session Highs and Lows
  • Previous Day Highs and Lows
  • Asia Highs and Lows

Because we trade in New York session, we use and look for London highs and lows, and Asian highs and lows as our draw on liquidity.

Finding Daily Bias: The 3 Situations

Situation 1: London Manipulation, New York Reversal

London takes out previous day highs or lows at around 8:30 hrs, and we start seeing a reversal of that in New York session. New York then starts aiming for key levels like London lows, Previous Day lows, and/or higher timeframe imbalances.

Situation 2: London Reversal, New York Continuation

When London session takes previous day highs and starts to reverse before going into New York. New York continues in the direction London reversed to, taking out low resistance liquidity and previous day lows.

Situation 3: London Consolidation, New York Manipulation/Extension

London consolidates, then when New York session opens, we get a manipulation taking out a previous day low or high. Then target/look for the reversal, or previous day highs/Asia lows. This includes the Asia session influence.

Key Levels for All Situations

  • London Lows
  • Previous Day Highs
  • Asia Lows
  • Previous Day Lows

When trying to find these, look on the 1hr timeframe.

Equilibrium (Detailed)

Helps us identify the premium and discounted price from swing highs to swing lows. There are 2 lots of internal liquidity found within the equilibrium.

If I see on the 1 hour timeframe that we have already found equilibrium, I would be looking for either longs or shorts depending on which direction price is going to become discounted. Also going to FVGs to find internal liquidity.

What is Invalidation of EQ?

If price pushes past either the high or the low of the EQ, we are no longer looking for that discounted price range.

2 Ways to Use EQ

  1. TJR method: When looking for EQ, use it on the previous high and low. Good, but doesn't catch the whole move.
  2. Eyesonly method: Use it to find the highest point of the full move, the highest point of the push down, and the lowest.

Stop Losses and Take Profits

Stop Losses

Placed where our trade idea is invalidated and/or FVG is disrespected.

  • If taking a stop off a FVG: place stop loss on the candle's wick at the top or at the bottom of the first candle's wick, depending on order flow
  • Break of Structure Stop Loss: If we have taken a trade at equilibrium, place stop loss at a spot that would be a higher low or break of structure point

Extra Knowledge

NDOG / NWOG (New Day / New Week Opening Gaps)

New Day Opening Gap and New Week Opening Gap: Opening gaps are the strongest draws on liquidity in the marketplace.

New Week Opening Gap: Close of previous week and open of the next week.

New Day Opening Gap: Same thing, between days not weeks.

FVG is a strong confluence because price has only been traded in one direction. Opening gaps are when they haven't been traded in any direction. It just makes sense to trade on these gaps.

FVG Tips

  1. Wait for FVG at a key level. Wait for price to re-enter it. If it starts to push back, it is a signal to enter.
  2. FVG at key level, then starts truly, price increases. It raises to lots of sideways, good. If bearish FVG: looking for longs. If bullish FVG: looking for shorts.

Finding Patterns
